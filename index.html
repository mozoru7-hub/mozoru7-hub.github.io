Smart Weight Device Production Line

A simulated industrial production line that assembles, checks, measures, and stores weight-device products using Python automation, a live HMI, and monitoring tools.

Project overview

From raw parts to inspected weight devices

The project models a complete manufacturing workflow for digital weight devices. Each product moves through stations for material loading, assembly, calibration, weight verification, quality control, packaging, and storage. The goal is to show how software can control and monitor a production line in a clear, realistic way.

01

Material loading

Base plates, sensor modules, displays, and housings enter the line as separate parts.

02

Assembly

The system combines the housing, load-cell sensor, electronics, and display into one device.

03

Calibration

Known reference values are used to prepare the device for accurate measurement.

04

Weight test

The product is tested against simulated measurement data to detect incorrect readings.

05

Quality decision

Passed products move forward. Failed products are rejected and counted by the system.

06

Packaging and storage

Approved devices are packaged, logged, and added to the final production output.

HMI and monitoring

Operator control and live feedback

The HMI gives the operator buttons and status information for starting, stopping, resetting, and observing the line. Monitoring dashboards can show accepted products, rejected products, cycle count, and simulated measurement values.
